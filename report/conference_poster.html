Food Nutrition Estimation from a Single RGB Image

Depth-aware geometric learning with direct and weight-first nutrient prediction for practical meal analysis.

Problem and Goal

Visual appearance alone is a weak proxy for mass and nutrition. Similar-looking dishes can have very different density and calorie content. We target practical, single-image estimation with uncertainty-aware output.

Main Contributions

  • Depth-aware 9D geometric descriptor from YOLOv8 segmentation and MiDaS depth.
  • Two nutrient paths: direct nutrient regression and weight-first conversion.
  • Dish classification prior (EfficientNet-B0) with optional USDA density lookup.
  • MC Dropout uncertainty reporting integrated into the user interface.
  • Editable Gradio workflow for human-in-the-loop correction.

Data and Protocol

Targets: calories, fat, protein, carbohydrate.

Evaluation split: 2597 train / 324 val / 326 test (fixed across comparisons).

Primary metrics: MAE and RMSE (MAPE used only as secondary signal).

Phased Development

  • Phase 3: ResNet-50 direct baseline.
  • Phase 4: Geometry-based MLP nutrient regression.
  • Phase 5: EfficientNet-B0 Food-101 classification.
  • Phase 6: Weight-first MLP then macro conversion.

Method

Model Details

Complementary Nutrient Paths (Phases 4 & 6)

From the 9D geometric feature vector g, two nutrient estimation paths are computed:

(2)  n̂ = fθ(g)
(3)  ŵ = hφ(g),  n̂j = ŵ ρj

Eq. (2) is the direct nutrient regression path (Phase 4). Eq. (3) is the weight-first conversion path (Phase 6), where per-gram constants ρj convert predicted weight to macronutrients.

MLP Architecture (Phases 4 & 6)

  • Input: 9 geometric features
  • Hidden layers: 128 → 64 → 32
  • Regularization: BatchNorm + ReLU + Dropout (0.2)
  • Output: 4 nutrients — calories, fat, protein, carb (Phase 4) or 1 scalar weight in grams (Phase 6)

Uncertainty and Deployment

Inference uses MC Dropout with repeated stochastic forward passes. The app reports mean prediction and confidence intervals, and propagates weight uncertainty to nutrient uncertainty in the weight-first path.

App inference sampling T = 30
Notebook evaluation sampling T = 50
Reported interval mu ± sigma, mu ± 2 sigma
Fallback safety low-confidence labels suppressed

Integrated Pipeline

Inference merges direct nutrient prediction, weight-first conversion, and dish-class priors into one editable interface.

Error Patterns

  • Mixed dishes with overlapping components.
  • Strong occlusion or unusual camera viewpoints.
  • Foods with similar geometry but different density.

Conclusion

The final integrated system balances predictive accuracy and interpretability by combining segmentation, depth cues, direct nutrient regression, weight-first reasoning, and uncertainty-aware reporting in a practical Gradio workflow.
